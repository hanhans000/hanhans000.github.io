Design and Layout

What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?


The first thing that stands out to me is the clean, minimalist design. This design choice is not only aesthetic but also functional. It creates a spacious and uncluttered environment. The space allows the user to concentrate on the important elements presented. The minimalist approach ensures that the 3D environment is the focal point and it immerses me from the beginning. The responsive animations is so energetic and interactive, they react intuitively to cursor movements and it helps to bring the experience to life. The smooth transitions between sections add fluidity to the experience making it a modern, dynamic feel. The most impressive to me is in my firsts interaction, this combination of design elements already allows the website to set the tone for what users can expect as they navigate more and further.

What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?


I spent the most time examining the various areas, including "Omada," "Coach+," and "Spark." Each of these parts is thoughtfully constructed with animated features intended to keep the user interested. The website's design leads users through various parts with interactive prompts and visual clues. Each part represents its own mini-universe filled with distinct clickable components and animations that provide fresh experiences with each interaction. Almost as if they are going through a digital world with something new around every corner. Sub-interactive components, such as mini-games or minor interactive tools, provide an additional layer of engagement.

What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?


The most common action was clicking on various elements to explore around the site. Every click or scroll reveals new content or animations, making it the primary mode of interaction. As I read on about Luni.app, the website is intended to guide users through exploration by encouraging them to click on various areas to learn more about the Luni ecosystem.

User Experience and Overall Impression

What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

Personally, I think the interactive experience appears to have two primary goals. First, it seeks to visually engage and immerse visitors in Luni's innovative apps. The website is intended to capture the essence of their work by providing a distinct digital area that represents their brand identity. The use of 3D environments, interactive components, and dynamic animations all help to lure the user into Luni's universe. Second, the site functions as a recruitment tool. It is encouraging people to join the Luni team by immersing them in its culture. The website's goal is to make users feel connected to the brand and ready to interact or join their team.

How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

This purpose is communicated through the website. Each component is designed to highlight distinct aspects of Luni's offers. It filled with 3D surroundings, seamless navigation, and compelling animations. The site's design does more than just tell the audience about Luni's offerings but also displays them. I think they are trying to potray their brand's identity as a pioneer in immersive online experiences and Luni is committed to developing creative, user-friendly digital solutions that are both functional and eye-catching.

What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time?

The experience is maybe captivating enough to keep users interested for a short time but I don't think it last for long-term interaction. The different sections and interactive elements encourage users to revisit and explore further so that explains why the overall interaction may be more about the short interactions, repeated sessions rather than longer interactions. I got a bit bored if I spend more time in here. However, I believe it is intended to be reviewed more from time to time to introduced users new content and updates and longer interactions in the future.

I think the experience references the metaverse and similar digital environments as well as app-based ecosystems and other digital solutions. These references bring out the interconnected nature of the experience, which, in my point of view, aligns with current trends in digital innovation and user engagement.

What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

The most frustrating part of interacting is the lack of detailed information in certain sections. While the website is visually appealing and engaging, I feel like it frequently sacrifices depth for aesthetics. This can lead to impatience, as users looking for more in-depth content or explanations may find themselves constantly clicking and searching for information that isn't fully available. The balance between form and function, for me, is not good enough. Through discovering the website, I feel discouraged and bored for seeking more about the website after such a short time.

What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The most satisfying aspect for me is the sleek, user-friendly layout and the clear and pleasing presentation of visual information, and that is also the reason why I chose this website at the beginning. The site's straightforward navigation and the way it encourages people to explore make it enjoyable to interact with. The responsive animations and the fact that each section of the site feels like a mini-experience and space does add a lot to the overall satisfaction.
